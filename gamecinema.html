Is Cinema Just a Game?

Has cinema really become just a game? Tempting to argue either way: on the one hand cinema hasn't really changed that much: classical cinema is still created, think of Selma, Lincoln and Detroit.

On the other hand, taking cinema as a whole:

  • Increasingly 'game inspired' visuals
  • Greater focus on unrealistic plots
  • Enormous emphasis on interaction through social media

Warren Buckland's argument is that film has evolved. Following on from what Aristotle called the complex plot, there is a new mode of story telling that actively obscures continuity and linearity.

The use of the term “complex” in Puzzle Films: Complex Storytelling in Contemporary Cinema extends far beyond Aristotle’s term peplegmenos. The “puzzle plot” is, I would argue, the third type of plot that comes after the complex plot. A puzzle plot is intricate in the sense that the arrangement of events is not just complex, but complicated and perplexing; the events are not simply interwoven, but entangled.

There are many examples of these 'entangled' plots, wherein complexity is not simply a result of subject matter but form.

  • Memento
  • Fight Club
  • Inception
  • Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind
  • Sans Soleil
  • Pi
  • The Fall
  • The Fountain
  • Primer
  • Upstream Colour

But what does this prevalence of entangled plots mean? I suggest three possibilities:

  • that views are more distracted and need to be engaged in increasingly active ways
  • that viewers, on the whole, want more challenging narratives as a form of 'attraction'
  • that viewers are more educated and divided so require narratives that they can resolve themselves
